Atlas of Weak Signals

 

Image Description

Process of building my initial design space

Research allows ideas that wouldn't be normally considered.
This process helped me understand links between specific topics that I wouldn't expect. Those links can build bridges for new ways of intervening and thinking as a designer.

It also gave me the opportunity to discover subjects that were knew to me and create new interests.

I defined the subjects I had more knowledge on or was interested to explore. I would say that the main subjects that came out the most often in my personal analysis are technology for equality, climate conscience, and refugee crisis




Weak Signal card game with the class

The whole class participated in the "Weak Signal" card game. We were all able to collectively make links between certain topics and share ideas.





 

Weak Signal card game played in a small group

We played the Weak Signal card game in a group exercise with Manuja, Dhrishya and Carlotta. At first, it was fun to discover all of our different knowledge on the topics written on the cards. The two cards we picked for weak signals were ‘gender fluidity’ and ‘circular data economy’. We defined both terms and started linking them to subjects such as ‘information sharing, safe spaces, and inclusive design’. Our two ‘area of opportunity’ cards were ‘co-creation’ and ‘coding’.

‘Gender fluidity’ and ‘co-creation’ were connected through the idea of going into communities to create a deeper understanding and inclusivity among different groups. ‘Circular Data Economy was put in relation with ‘coding’ due to its link with open source projects. We linked ‘co-creation’ with ‘macrobiotic literacy’, being able to integrate ‘macrobiotic literacy’ into our day-to-day life.

‘Coding’ and ‘macrobiotic literacy’ could also be linked through the study of genetic code or bio-hacking.

This exercise was very interesting in creating unexpected links between subjects that are dear to our hearts.





Weak Signal initial design space

In summary

In summary, the connections between "Technology for Equality" and "Climate Consciousness" underscore the importance of equitable resource access and raising awareness through technology. The integration of technology equality with maker education could lead to a more inclusive and accessible approach, positively impacting digital gender disparities and empowering underrepresented communities. Additionally, the fusion of climate consciousness with computational thinking and design offers ideas for addressing pressing issues. These linkages, alongside connections to local material recirculation and regenerative design, collectively present a comprehensive framework for a more sustainable and equitable future.

Creating all these links offers us a panel of topics and directions to explore throughout the year. These are all challenging and complex issues to tackle but it is very exciting. :) 

Map of Weak Signal threw multiscalar perspective 

Weak Signal final design space for now*

Sharing our ‘personal Design Spaces’ in small groups

Sharing our ‘personal Design Spaces’ enabled us to understand personal points of view, common interests, and knowledge on specific subjects such as local material recirculation, maker education, food crisis, and refugee crisis.

Suggestions for the deck

- Connectivity for displaced populations and communities: linking refugee tech with design for social innovation, and maker education.
- Disruptive innovation technology: linking it to AI, climate conscience, and circular economy.
- Emergency architecture systems and design: linking it to refugee tech, digital fabrication, and co-creation.
