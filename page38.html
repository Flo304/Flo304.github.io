Self evaluation

- Relevance of the interventions concerning weak signals and the state of the art
I believe that most of my interventions generally stayed within the scopes of my initial weak signals. I found them to be a good introduction to each topic, especially since I had less knowledge about some of them initially. My initial weak signals were related to climate conscience, inter-species collaboration, regenerative design, technology for equality, non-hetero patriarchal innovation, refugee tech, open-source, and rural futures. These signals then evolved into three specific topics that I would like to explore further: climate conscience, knowledge for all, and inter-species collaboration.

During the interventions, I explored specific topics within these broader categories. For instance, I focused on energy consumption (PowerPlay), which is related to climate conscience, and electronics and knowledge for all. The project Micro to Macro addressed inter-species collaboration, knowledge for all, and climate conscience. Finally, the project on public transportation in Barcelona was linked to the weak signal of technology for equality. Each intervention not only raised new questions but also allowed me to choose which subjects I wanted to investigate more thoroughly. It also provided an opportunity to put into practice the new knowledge I acquired, such as using prototyping roles to research subjects and frame interventions.

When reflecting on the approach I am taking to design during the interventions, I aspire to evolve in the way I approach my work. Knowledge can be acquired in various ways, as demonstrated through the roles of prototyping and other examples. I believe there is room for improvement in detaching myself further from the traditional education system, where I often find myself hiding behind my computer during research. I am conscious of the fortunate situation we have in Barcelona, with vibrant communities that promote ideas aligned with my own. Consequently, I aim to challenge myself to be more active on the social side of my pursuits.

In the second term, I aim to expand my knowledge of electronics and learn how to integrate them into my projects. I want to ask myself the right questions and align them with my values. As Guillem wisely pointed out, I aim to "not sprinkle electronics on a project" but rather integrate them thoughtfully. ;)

- Framing the interventions and their development within the evolution of the Design Space
Framing interventions and their development within the context of the Design Space is an intriguing journey. The beauty of the design space lies in its ability to grant you the freedom to explore diverse topics and uncover their interconnectedness without excessive pressure. Drawing from my personal experience, I view the Design Space as a tool for structuring the trajectory of my research.
Within this framework, you have the flexibility to zoom in or out of your design space, enabling the discovery of new perspectives. The Design Space, in its evolution, serves as a constant reminder of the values I aim to integrate into my projects.
In essence, framing interventions and their development within the evolving Design Space offers a method to organise my ideas effectively, providing a guideline for my research.

I was a bit scared of the complexity of the interconnection between subjects and seeing how they fit in the interventions. Thanks to the research and social interactions that I had, I'm slowly taking a grasp of how to deal with this overflow of information. One key element to improve would definitely be reading more about the subjects I'm trying to investigate. 


- Involvement of the community through the design interventions
Reflecting on the first term, I realise that I could have engaged more with communities beyond IAAC. Whether due to time constraints or social anxiety, it's crucial for me to step out of my comfort zone. Embracing this new practice, I'm learning to integrate community involvement into my projects. Moving forward, I plan to better organise my time to create opportunities for these interactions.

Interacting with communities can be intimidating, especially when I might possess less knowledge on the subject, and I want to avoid appearing disrespectful. Striking the right balance is essential, and it's okay (though not ideal, I do my best) to feel a bit uneasy during initial contacts with a community. As emphasised in the Design Studio, I need to find my boundaries and equilibrium without, at the same time, missing out on opportunities to connect with these communities.

I perceive our MDEF and IAAC class as a community. Looking at it from a different perspective, each member of our group, including teachers, was highly engaged during the interventions I participated in. In our groups, there were people interested in similar things. We all shared different ideas and helped spread knowledge among each other.

In summary, I'd rate myself at 75%. This assessment considers various factors, including attendance, meeting deadlines, active participation in group activities, decision-making within groups, curiosity, listening skills, knowledge exchange, public speaking, relevance of research, skills acquired, and involvement in the MDEF community. While there are areas for improvement, my primary focus is to shift my work approach, believing it will positively impact many of these factors. I aim to produce fewer but more valuable pieces of work and to increase my interactions with people and communities in general.
